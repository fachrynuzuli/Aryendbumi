#### want to get ahead? give credit where it's due!
*06 Sep, 2024* 

For every successful event I've ever experienced in my careerhood, one quote has been the lifeblood of my career: **"Credit where the credit is due."**

Recognizing other's efforts can incrementally increases our chance of success. 
Here's how it works:

**Recognizing others build trust among team members.** When you acknowledge other's hard work publicly, it's a sign that you know and value their contributions. Like in a team meeting, highlighting your peer's significant role in a project will almost always build mutual respect. This, will promote a culture of openness.

**Recognition encourages colaboration.** Collaboration thrives in an environment of mutual respect. By recognizing them regularly, you create an atmosphere where others feel comfortable sharing ideas working together. I claim to 10x'ed overall team performance just by perform this correctly, carefully, and intentionally.

**Giving credit help builds positive work environment.** When you make it a habit to give credit, you contribute to a supportive and motivating workplace. A simple act of appreciation towards colleague for their work can make a significant difference. This approach not only boosts team morale but also increase the sense of belonging and purpose beneath the group.

Work is a place where you learn how to teach others without teaching. Tell others without telling, and ask others without asking.
